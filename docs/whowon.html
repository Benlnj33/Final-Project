Who Won?: Microsoft




Netscape filed a seven-count suit charging Microsoft for harming Netscape in a series of illegal acts aimed at promoting Microsoft's Internet Explorer browser at the expense of Netscape Navigator.


Though Netscape won the lawsuit and Microsoft ultimately had to pay Netscape/AOL $750,000,000, Microsoft was too dominant as a web browser and owned the market causing Netscape to be bought by AOL.


"Microsoft took steps to bring Netscape down not just by developing its own version of a web browser called internet explorer but then also technologically and contractually tied Internet Explorer to its monopoly Windows operating system. They ensured that only Internet Explorer ran well on Windows and Microsoft designed Windows."
